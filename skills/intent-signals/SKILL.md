# Intent Signals

Map the most relevant buying signals for a specific market and ICP. Buying signals are observable events or behaviors that indicate a company is likely to need your solution. The difference between spray-and-pray outreach and precision targeting is signal infrastructure.

This skill produces a prioritized signal map, defines how to capture each signal, and creates messaging angles that connect each signal to a relevant outreach conversation.

## Inputs

- **ICP description** (required) — who the ideal customer is (industry, size, role, pain points)
- **Product/service description** (required) — what you sell and what problem it solves
- **Current signal sources** (optional) — any signals already being tracked
- **Budget context** (optional) — helps calibrate capture method recommendations (free tools vs. paid platforms)

## Steps

### Step 1 — Understand the ICP and Buying Triggers

Read the provided ICP description and product context. Identify:
- What event or change typically creates the need for this product?
- What does the buyer's world look like right before they start evaluating solutions?
- What internal or external pressures drive purchasing decisions?

These "trigger moments" are the foundation for signal mapping.

### Step 2 — Research Signal Categories

For the specific ICP and market, evaluate relevance across these 8 signal categories:

1. **Hiring signals** — job postings for roles that indicate a relevant initiative (e.g., hiring a "Head of Revenue Operations" signals CRM/tooling investment). Which job titles, which keywords in descriptions?

2. **Funding signals** — recent funding rounds that create budget and growth pressure. Which stages matter most? Series A companies behave differently from Series D.

3. **Technology adoption signals** — new tools being adopted that indicate a shift (e.g., adopting Salesforce suggests they're building a sales team). Check job postings for tech stack clues, BuiltWith, or similar.

4. **Content engagement signals** — engagement with relevant content topics (webinar attendance, whitepaper downloads, blog readership). What topics signal buying intent vs. casual interest?

5. **Job change signals** — when a known champion or decision-maker moves to a new company. This is one of the highest-conversion signals available.

6. **Expansion signals** — new office openings, geographic expansion, team growth visible on LinkedIn. What scale of expansion matters?

7. **Regulatory/compliance signals** — new regulations, policy changes, or compliance deadlines that create urgency. Which regulations are relevant?

8. **Competitive displacement signals** — visible dissatisfaction with a competitor (negative reviews, public complaints, contract renewal periods). Where do these surface?

### Step 3 — Prioritize by Relevance and Actionability

Not all signals are equally useful. Score each signal category on two axes:
- **Relevance** (1-5) — how strongly does this signal correlate with buying intent for this specific ICP?
- **Actionability** (1-5) — how easy is it to capture this signal and act on it with available resources?

Rank signals by combined score. The top 3-5 signals become the primary focus.

### Step 4 — Define Capture Methods

For each prioritized signal, define exactly how to capture it:
- **Data source** — where does this signal surface? (LinkedIn, job boards, Crunchbase, G2, Google Alerts, industry publications)
- **Capture method** — manual monitoring, automated alerts, paid tool, or custom scraping
- **Frequency** — how often to check (daily, weekly, event-triggered)
- **Tool recommendations** — specific tools that can automate capture (free and paid options)

Be practical. Start with what can be done manually or cheaply, then layer in automation.

### Step 5 — Create Messaging Angles Per Signal

For each prioritized signal, write a messaging angle that connects the signal to a relevant outreach conversation. The structure:

- **Signal observed:** [What you noticed]
- **Why it matters to them:** [What this signal implies about their situation]
- **Bridge to your value:** [How your solution connects to this moment]
- **Opening line example:** [One sentence that could open a cold email or LinkedIn message]

The key is relevance. The prospect should feel like you understand their situation, not like you scraped a database.

### Step 6 — Create Operationalization Roadmap

Define a phased rollout:
- **Week 1-2:** Set up capture for the top 2 signals using free/manual methods
- **Week 3-4:** Build messaging templates for each signal, test with initial outreach
- **Month 2:** Add next 2 signals, evaluate conversion rates from signal-based outreach vs. cold
- **Month 3:** Automate capture where ROI justifies tooling investment

## Quality Checks

- At least 6 of the 8 signal categories are evaluated, even if some are marked as low-relevance.
- Top 3-5 signals have complete capture method definitions. No signal is prioritized without a concrete way to capture it.
- Every messaging angle includes a specific opening line, not just a generic template.
- Compound signals are identified. Single signals are useful; combinations are powerful.
- The roadmap starts with free/manual methods. Do not recommend expensive tooling as Step 1.
- Signal definitions are specific to this ICP. "Hiring signals" alone is too vague. "Posting for a Director of Demand Gen with Marketo experience" is specific.
